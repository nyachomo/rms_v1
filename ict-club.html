Our Mission

Empowering High School Students Through Code

Techsphere Digital Skills Academy has partnered with high schools across Kenya to establish structured ICT Clubs — dedicated spaces where students learn to code, build digital solutions, and discover the world of technology during their weekly ICT club sessions.

The Process

How the ICT Club Works

From onboarding your school to running weekly sessions, here is how our partnership unfolds.

1

School Partnership

Your school signs a partnership agreement with Techsphere to establish an official ICT Club, open to all Form 1–4 students.

2

Club Registration

Interested students are registered as ICT Club members. We provide registration forms, membership cards, and a welcome kit.

3

Weekly Sessions

Our trainers visit on the school's designated ICT club day to deliver structured, practical coding lessons aligned to each term.

4

Certification

Students who complete the programme receive a Techsphere-certified completion certificate, recognised for university applications.

Curriculum

What Students Learn

Our ICT Club curriculum is designed for beginners, progressing term by term from foundational concepts to real projects.

Term 1 — Digital Foundations

Students get introduced to computers, the internet, and the basics of building web pages.

  • Computer basics & digital literacy
  • Introduction to HTML & CSS
  • Building a simple personal webpage
  • Internet safety & responsible use

Term 2 — Introduction to Coding

Students begin writing real code using Scratch and Python, developing logic and problem-solving skills.

  • Block-based coding with Scratch
  • Python basics — variables & loops
  • Simple game & animation projects
  • Debugging & computational thinking

Term 3 — Build & Showcase

Students apply everything learned to build a real project and present it at the end-of-year ICT Club Showcase.

  • Designing a mini web application
  • Introduction to UI/UX thinking
  • Team project development
  • Project presentation & showcase
